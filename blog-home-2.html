The Impact of Climate Change on Biodiversity

Climate change has emerged as one of the greatest threats to biodiversity worldwide. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and extreme weather events directly impact ecosystems and disrupt the delicate balance of species and habitats. As temperatures increase, many species are forced to migrate in search of suitable climates, altering the distribution and composition of ecosystems. This disruption can lead to a loss of biodiversity as some species struggle to adapt or face increased competition for resources. Additionally, climate change affects the timing of key biological events, such as flowering, breeding, and migration, which can cause mismatches with dependent species and disrupt critical ecological relationships.
