Understanding Carbon Footprint: What It Is and Why It Matters

A carbon footprint is a measure of the impact human activities have on the environment in terms of greenhouse gas emissions. It takes into account the burning of fossil fuels for energy, transportation, industrial processes, deforestation, and other factors that contribute to the release of CO2 and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. The carbon footprint is usually measured in tons of CO2 equivalent (CO2e) per year.
